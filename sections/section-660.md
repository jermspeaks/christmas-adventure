# Controlling Joy

You moved through the celebration, trying to guide it, to control it, to make sure everything was perfect. *If I can just make sure everyone celebrates the right way,* you thought, *then everything will be perfect.*

But as you tried to control things, you began to notice tension. People were looking at you with uncertainty, with confusion. The celebration that had been joyful and free was becoming... constrained. Controlled. Less joyful.

*This isn't right,* you realized, watching as people's expressions shifted, as the joy began to fade, replaced by uncertainty. *I'm trying to control the celebration, but celebration isn't meant to be controlled. It's meant to be free, to flow, to be joyful.*

You saw Elara watching you, her expression concerned. "Are you okay?" she asked. "You seem... tense. Like you're trying to control everything. But the celebration is fine. It's joyful. It's working. You don't need to control it."

*She's right,* you thought. *I'm trying to control something that doesn't need to be controlled. The celebration is fine. It's joyful. It's working. I'm just making it worse.*

Cheshire trotted over to you, their eyes gentle. "Celebration isn't meant to be controlled," they said. "It's meant to be free, to flow, to be joyful. When you try to control it, you take away its joy, its freedom, its magic. Sometimes the best thing you can do is let go, let it flow, let it be what it is."

The Keepsake Keeper pulsed softly. "Joy can't be controlled," it said. "It can't be forced, can't be directed. It needs to flow freely, to be shared, to be experienced. When you try to control it, you take away its essence, its magic, its joy."

*So I need to let go,* you thought. *I need to stop trying to control the celebration, to let it flow, to let it be joyful. I need to trust that it will be okay, that it doesn't need my control.*

You looked around, at the celebration, at the people, at the joy. *It's fine,* you realized. *It's working. It's joyful. I don't need to control it. I just need to let it be.*

What do you do?
