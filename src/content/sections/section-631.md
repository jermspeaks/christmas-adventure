# Patient Teaching

You took a deep breath and stepped back. *I need to slow down,* you thought. *I need to teach in smaller groups, one thing at a time.*

"Let's break this into smaller groups," you said to the gathered crowd. "I'll work with a few people at a time, and we'll learn together, step by step."

You worked through the groups slowly, patiently, helping each person understand one concept before moving to the next. And as you did, you saw the understanding growing. People weren't overwhelmed anymore. They were learning, step by step, piece by piece.

But as you worked, you began to notice something. Some people were still struggling, even in the smaller groups. They needed more help, more guidance, more time. And you were only one person, trying to teach everyone.

*I'm making progress,* you thought. *But there are still people who need help. I can't reach everyone at once, even in smaller groups.*
